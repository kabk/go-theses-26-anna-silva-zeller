Out of an appreciation and respect for our non-human kin and a desire for more caring ways of being, I started to collect moments of interaction between us and those we share this earth with. In an extensive list, I gradually combine and track information I’ve stumbled upon, heard or known, sought out, researched, experienced, read, or felt. The gathered contents are rooted in journalism, science, literature, social media and art. Repetition, rhythm, and familiarity give shape to the hard facts and frame the personal story of a lost opportunity at a family farm. This paper functions as an anchor for my artistic project, acting as a counterweight that allows me to explore gentler connections. It understands itself as an exhausted sigh in the long-known, holding what’s uncomfortable to see. It repeats and ruminates as a sort of chant, holding grief to affirm reorientation.
