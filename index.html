30% of all landmass is used for livestock.

Every 1.2 seconds, one football pitch of forest is cut.

In July 2017, an old camphor tree inside Arisugawa-no-Miya Park was moved by 10m to make room for road construction.

A group of students in Zurich take up anew the act of picking linden blossoms for tea.

Through the previous abandonment of the act of picking, the trees have been rendered invisible.

A first version of the chainsaw was invented around 250 years ago as a medical instrument.

In Taoist tradition, you must warn a tree two weeks before cutting it down.

In 1996, it took a worker using traditional felling methods 18 minutes to cut down a tree in the Brazilian Amazon.

Using “modern techniques of environmentally sound harvesting”, it took them 22 minutes.

Depending on the circumference of the stem, in 2010, a high-powered harvester needed 1-2 minutes to fell a tree.

A writer thinks about revisiting the tree whose root she fell over and broke her wrist, to beg for a little solace.

Death by bolt is considered the most humane way of killing an animal.

Within 2 milliseconds, the bolt causes a severe concussion at impact and massive over- and underpressure in the brain when entering and exiting the head. Due to strong rowing movements of their limbs, which endanger staff and make rapid and safe exsanguination harder and the difficulty of tying them down tightly enough to guarantee precise positioning of the apparatus, bolt killing is mostly prohibited with pigs.

Suicide rates among slaughterhouse workers are higher than in any other sector. An artist writes a letter to a dead pigeon she finds on the street.
